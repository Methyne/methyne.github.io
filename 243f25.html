Accessing Materials: Grades can be viewed on Canvas, homeworks are done via WebAssign, announcements are via Canvas, and all other materials are on this page. This includes discussion worksheets, solutions to (worksheets, quizzes, exams), class overview, summary of what happens each day, and extra files.

Before Discussion: Try the problems on the discussion worksheet before class. Worksheets can be downloaded here at least 2 days before the discussion.

Discussion Agenda: Once discussion starts, you can ask questions about any problems you had trouble with. You can also ask about any topics in the problems that was confusing or didn't seem to be covered by prior materials. If there are no more questions, I'll select 1-2 additional problems to cover before ending. When I go over the solution to a problem, I pause after a crucial step to give students chances to ask for further clarification before moving onto the next step. On days with a quiz, it happens at the end of discussion for the last 20 minutes.

Worksheet Info: Worksheets are posted (ignoring the 1st week of classes hiccup) at least 2 days in advance of discussion. Solutions are posted the end of Thursday the same week. The solution document may be dry compared to the richness of talking through the solution and every step in discussion, so email me if you missed discussion, are reading a solution in the document, and don't understand some step or the motivation to take that step.

Homework Info: Homeworks are done on WebAssign. They are automatically graded and grades appear in Canvas. If you have any trouble with the site, contact me or the instructor. Many issues with the WebAssign not accepting an answer can often be resolved by checking for syntax and type errors.

Quiz Info: There will be a quiz in discussion every [X] weeks taking place in person during the last [Y] minutes of the discussion. Every quiz is a mix of short answer, long answer, and calculation questions. Legible scratch work will be used to award partial credit for any question.

Exam Info: Midterms are in person during your lecture time. The final exam date and time is TBD. Tentative Times: Midterm 1 is instructor TBD, midterm 2 is instructor TBD, final is registrar TBD. Exams are no calculator, no items, final destination only. Midterm 2 covers only material between it and midterm 1, but the final is cumulative. If you have questions about the grading of the exams, contact the instructor. When I pass back graded exams or quizzes during discussions, you have until you leave the classroom to mention any concerns and must pass back the papers for a regrade.

Makeups: For exam makeups, contact the instructor as soon as you know you're going to miss the exam; the earlier, the better. Similarly, contact me for discussion quiz makeups. Contact the instructor about homework deadlines.

Logistical remarks: The last half of Multivariable Calculus is jam packed with new concepts and tricky problems. If you fall behind in September or October, you have ample time, but if you fall behind in November or December, it will be hard to catch up. To avoid procrastinating, start the homework and worksheets early.
